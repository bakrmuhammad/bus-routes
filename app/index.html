The Miami Herald took a look at the bus routes of Miami, and the complaints that come with them.

What's wrong with the busiest bus routes in Miami-Dade?

34 bus routes account for 75% of rides in Miami-Dade Transit. The also have 62% of all complaints.

Below are the various types of complaints along with the total number of complaints and comments for the busiest bus routes in Miami-Dade County.
On the map, click on the categories in order to see which routes have the highest rates of complaints per 100,000 rides. Hover or tap on the routes for more details.
Schedule
Issues with busses not arriving on time.
Driver Complaints
Drivers accused of acting unprofessionally.
No Stop
Claims that drivers missed a waiting passenger or stop request.
Safety
Actions that allegedly put passengers or motorists in danger.
Miscellaneous
Complaints that don't fit into categories.
Bus Stop Issues
Problems including bench damage, faulty signs and the need for a new bus stop in an unserved location.
Praise
Pasengers don't just gripe. Drivers get credit for being hard-working, quick-thinking, and concientous.
Cleanliness
Complaints about dirty busses, from roaches to spilled coffee.
Capacity
Complaints about crowded busses, and routes that riders think may need frequent stops.
Maintenance
Mechanical problems and other equipment woes.
Fares
Issues with paying for a bus ride.
Passenger Conduct
When passengers misbehave, other passengers complain.
